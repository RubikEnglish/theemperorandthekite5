"The Emperor and the Kite"

"El Emperador y la Cometa"




41. The evil men ruled with their cruel, harsh ways, and the people of the country were very sad.



42. One day, as the princess prepared a basket of food for her father, the old monk passed by her hut. She smiled at him, but he seemed not to see her.



43. Yet, as he passed, he repeated his prayer in a loud voice. He said:



44. My kite sails upward. Mounting to the high heavens. My emperor goes on wings.



45. The princess started to thank him. But then she stoped. Something was different. The words were not quite right.



46. “Stop,” she called to the monk. But he had already passed by. He was a monk, after all, and did not take part in things of this world.



47. And then Djeow Seow understood. The monk was telling her something important. And she understood.



48. Each day after that, when she was not bringing food to her father, Djeow Seow was busy.



49. She twined a string of grass and vines, and wove in strands of her own long black hair.



50. When her rope was as thick as her waist and as high as the tower, she was ready.
